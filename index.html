    What is fudge?

    Fudge is a type of delicious, delicious dessert. I personally have never tasted fudge before, but I have once sampled Banana-Fudge ice cream,

    and have since then fell in love with it. Unfortunately, there are no Fudge stores around me, but I will be getting some soon! ;)

     

    Fudge is a dessert where you mix chocolate and other ingredients and heat it up to 114c. This will

    produce a hot, thick liquid, which after it cools down, turns hard and can be cut into squares. Chocolate is

    one of the primary and most classical flavours, but there are others as well, such as Tiger fluff, peanut butter, vanilla and maple!

     

    How to make ez chocolate fudge:

    Ingredients:

    -2 cups chocolate chips; semi-sweet or milk both work

    -14 ounces sweetened condensed milk

    -1 teaspoon vanilla extract

     

    Instructions:

    -Take a 8x8 inch baking pan (21.2 centimetres), and line it with baking parchment.

    -Combine sweetened condensed mikl with chocolate chips and vanilla extract and heat it up in a saucepan; alternetively,

    put in microwave and heat for 30 seconds, then take out and mix. repeat until mixture is smooth.

    -Pour in lined pan and smooth it out. Put in fridge and wait 2 hours

    ezezezezeezez fudge!

     

    Notes:

    Make sure to spread to edges as well

    You can swap vanilla extract for almond extract while making an almond flavoured one, as well as add assorted nuts to your fudge.

     

    How to make double-layered fudge:

    mmmmmmmmm double layerz

    Chocolate:

    Ingredients:

    -225g chocolate chips; semi sweet or milk

    -180g sweetened condensed milk

    -1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract

    -1/4 tablespoon of salted butter

     

    Instructions:

    Pour sweetened condensed milk and chocolate chips into bowl

    Put in microwave for 30 secs, then add vanilla extract and salted butter

    Mix until smooth and no imperfections remain. Pour into pan with baking parchment. Smooth out.

     

    White Chocolate:

    Ingredients:

    -225g white chocolate chunks/chips

    -180g sweetened condensed milk

    -1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract

    -1/2 tablespoon of salted butter

     

    Instructions:

    -Pour sweetened condensed milk and white chocolate chunks/chips into bowl

    -Put in microwave for 30 secs and stir, repeat until soft

    -Add vanilla extract and salted butter. Mix until smooth and no imperfections remain.

    -Pour above chocolate fudge. Decorate with sprinkles.

     

    Final touches:

    -Leave in fridge for 2-3 hours. Take out of pan, cut up

    -Double layered fudge Double layered fudge yayayyayaAYAYYAYAYAYAYYAY!

     

    Notes:

    Make sure to cover sides of pan with baking parchment as well

    Spread to all corneeers

    Make sure to flatten your chocolate fudge before pouring on white chocolate fudge.

    A breif history of fudge!

    The term "fudge" means "cobbled together" or "to fit together in a clumsy manner".

    Originating from the 17th century, It was used as slang back then by sailors

    to respond to untruth or nonsense.

     

    The actual desert was created in the late 19th century, somewhere in the 1880s. The

    story goes that a batch of caramels were "fudged"- made incorrectly- and resulted in a more

    soft, creamy, chewy texture that was different from other desserts at the time.

    The first documented creation of fudge was in the 1880s, where a student at a local girls

    university somehow got ahold of the recipie and made 30 pounds of it, which was sold at an auction.

     

    it's distinct texture and flavour, as well as It's inexpensive and simpleness, as it was made

    with only 3-4 ingredients, captivated people who looked for cheap, affordable and simple candy

    that they can make at home themselves. Specialised fudge shops opened in tourist hotspots, and

    the price of sugar was decreasing at that time, furthur spreading it's popularity. Soon, the creation

    spread worldwide, with people experimenting with new flavours, which lead to the sweet, delicious dessert

    we have today!
